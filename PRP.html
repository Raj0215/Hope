Advanced PRP +

What Is Advanced PRP +?

Advanced PRP therapy uses a derma pen, a microneedling tool, instead of a regular syringe to apply platelet-rich plasma (PRP). The derma pen creates controlled tiny punctures in the skin, which helps the PRP spread more evenly and be absorbed better. This approach tends to be less painful, speeds up recovery, and often leads to better results for skin rejuvenation and hair growth. By combining microneedling with PRP, the treatment promotes healing and takes advantage of the healing properties in your own blood for more effective results.

Benefits of Advanced PRP therapy

Enhanced Absorption: Tiny punctures made by the derma pen help PRP penetrate deeper and be absorbed more effectively.
Even Distribution: The derma pen provides uniform coverage, ensuring consistent PRP application across the treatment area.
Reduced Pain: Microneedling with a derma pen is generally less painful compared to traditional syringe injections.
Faster Recovery: Patients often experience quicker healing and less downtime.
Improved Results: The combined effect of microneedling and PRP can lead to better outcomes in skin rejuvenation and hair restoration.
Minimized Risk: The controlled nature of the derma pen reduces the risk of complications compared to syringe injections.

Experience Post-Treatment:


1-2 Months: Initial signs of improvement, such as reduced scalp sensitivity and minor changes in hair texture, may begin to appear.
2-3 Months: Early signs of hair growth, such as tiny new hairs or thicker hair strands, may start to become noticeable.
3-6 Months: Optimal results are typically visible as more significant hair regrowth and improved hair density are seen. Collagen production and hair follicle rejuvenation continue during this period.
Procedure Frequency: PRP sessions are usually scheduled every 1-2 months. The full treatment process may span up to 6 months, depending on the number of sessions recommended.
Ongoing Maintenance: Additional sessions might be needed for sustained results or further enhancement of hair growth.
