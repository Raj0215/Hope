Failed Hair Transplant Repair



What Is Failed Hair Transplant Repair?

Failed Hair Transplant Repair involves correcting or revising a hair transplant that did not meet expectations or resulted in unsatisfactory outcomes. This procedure aims to address issues such as unnatural hairlines, poor graft survival, or inadequate coverage from previous transplants.


Why Choose Failed Hair Transplant Repair?

  Assessment : Evaluate the failed transplant to identify issues like irregular hairlines or uneven density.

  Graft Removal or Adjustment : Remove or reposition problematic grafts to correct appearance and growth issues.

  Redesigning the Hairlinel : Create a new hairline design for improved aesthetics and better blending with existing hair.

  Re-Transplantation : Add new follicles to fill gaps and enhance density using advanced techniques

  Recovery and Growth : Focus on healing and follicle integration. New hair growth begins in a few months, with full results in 9 to 12 months.


Procedure

  Improved Appearance : The advanced techniques ensure a realistic hairline and seamless blending with existing hair.

  Enhanced Density : Fewer incisions and reduced trauma to the scalp lead to quicker recovery and minimal scarring.

  Customizable Solutions : Bio-enhancement methods improve the survival and growth rates of transplanted hair follicles.


Experience During The Procedure And Post-Procedure

The repair process usually takes several hours and involves minimal discomfort due to local anesthesia. Recovery involves managing mild swelling or discomfort, with most patients resuming normal activities within a few days. Full results become evident as new hair grows in over the following months.
